Adventure

The Himalayas is the Earth’s great summit. The grand northern peaks of India thread into wild Nepal and wind east through the vast, prehistoric knit of Bhutan and Tibet. Mystery, beauty and tradition are ingrained into the great lands of the Himalaya. While the ancient monasteries of Tibet emit a calming peace, Kathmandu hums with the life of its people. Meanwhile, the behemoth of Mount Everest stands guard over an immense natural landscape of forests, gorges and peaks. Real adventure flows through the region’s life-blood, while real stories of spirituality and survival are told through the land and its people.
Magnificent snow-capped mountain peaks, small villages and a smiling local people greet trekkers as they traverse the Himalayan foothills. For more adventure, treks to the Everest Base Camp (explorehimalaya.com) take visitors to the spot where mountain climbers begin their ascent of the world’s highest mountain, Mt. Everest.
Mountain biking offers an adventurous, eco-friendly way to experience the Himalayas. Since trails pass through several climate zones, bicyclists see different types of flora and fauna.

Wildlife

Himalayas are the longest as well as the highest mountain ranges of the world. One third of all mountain animals, including some of the most endangered species, have been found to inhabit the Himalayan ranges. Himalayan region of India serves as the natural habitat to a wide variety of wildlife.The Himalayan Mountains offers world’s most beautiful landscapes and provide shelter to some of the rarest wild animals, reptiles and birds. It is the longest and the highest mountain range that gives home to many endangered species some of which include Snow Leopard, Giant Panda, Red Panda, Himalayan Wild Yak, Himalayan Thar, Musk Dear, Himalayan Marmot, etc. In Sanskrit the Himalayas are known as the 'abode of snow' but they provide rich and varied habitats for the animals and plants that live there; from the forests of the foothills to ice-bound peaks and mountain lakes.

In the Terai region, the flora of the Himalayas comprises of Sal, Teak and Shisham/rosewood forests. As the altitudes increases, the terrain changes to include Chir (Pine), Oak, Deodar, Fir, Rhododendron, Birch and Juniper trees. One can also find some of the rarest flowers in the Himalayan region. As you explore the varied wildlife of the Himalayas you will get to know many interesting and amazing facts on the wildlife in the great Himalayas. The Himalayan grasslands have the densest population of Bengal tigers, which live alongside Asian elephants and one-horned rhinos. The mountains offer refuge for red pandas, golden langurs and takins. This is the only known location in the world where Bengal tigers and snow leopards share habitat.Most of the animals at Himalaya have thick skin and heavy body with phenomenal instinct that helps them protect their lives even in most arctic atmosphere.

Standing majestically on the banks of River Yamuna, the Taj Mahal is synonymous to love and romance. The name "Taj Mahal" was derived from the name of Shah Jahan's wife, Mumtaz Mahal, and means "Crown Palace".

Tiger's Nest

Paro Taktsang (Tiger's Nest) - the Breathtaking Himalayan Cloud Monastery. It is one of the most revered places of pilgrimage, with a founding shrouded in strange tales and legend. Built in the late 17th century, today, it is considered to be one of the holiest sites in Bhutan as well as a cultural icon that is open only once a year in a special ceremony
